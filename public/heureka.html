Eureka - bellowed infamously upon the discovery of gold - celebrates an insight made obvious only in hindsight.
A word so important that it emblazons California's state seal.

But eureka is neutered from its original meaning.
After Archimedes realized he could measure the volume of an irregular object (like a king's crown) by how much water it displaces, he leapt out of his bath and ran naked through the streets shouting not eureka - but heureka!
That h is significant, as it connects eureka's celebration to heuristic, the process which produced it.

Put another way: if eureka is the etymological equivalent of a coach lighting a victory cigar, a heuristic is his game plan.
Somewhere along the way the h, a letter which the Greeks called 'rough breath', disappeared, severing the eureka moment from the heuristic method that produced it.
That h, that rough breath, that is creative heuristics: reading support tickets to understand not just what your product does but why it matters, the project brief achieving consensus between the builders of your product and its sellers, the zero draft between writer and expert in which a seed of a eureka insight is planted - that is what marketing content has lost.
It was lost when content became a numbers game -- one in which metrics replaced methods, and people cared more about the numbers on the spreads.

And so the eureka insight that opens minds and closes deals became impossible to discover because companies were too busy producing content for its own sake.
Your content is the first - and perhaps only - salesperson your user meets.

And this is where a creative heuristic earns its value, by communicating the eureka insight which says what you need to say in a way no one ever thought of - until we did.
